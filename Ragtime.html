Ragtime

Ragtime is known for being a big influence on the development of Jazz. Like the blues, Ragtime was developed by African-American musicians. One of the most influential of these musicians was named Scott Joplin. Joplin is perhaps best remembered for a tune he composed called "Maple Leaf Rag."

Whereas the blues was created in the countryside, Ragtime was created in the city. Ragtime was heavily syncopated, which meant that the heavy beat of the music seemed as though it was "ragged." It is said that black musicians developed this upbeat, highly rhythmic music to entertain the customers who visited saloons and "sporting houses" (houses of prostitution). Ragtime, unlike classical music, did not seem to appeal to the listeners' heads; instead it appealed to their hearts and feet. It made people want to get up and dance!

Significant Ragtime Musicians

  • Scott Joplin
  • James Scott
  • Joseph Lamb
