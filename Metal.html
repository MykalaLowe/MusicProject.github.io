Metal

Heavy metal is a sub-genre of rock music that emerged as a defined musical style in the 1970s. Its roots are firmly entrenched in hard rock bands, which between 1969 and 1974, mixed blues and rock music, creating a thick, heavy, guitar-and-drums-centered sound characterized by the use of highly-amplified guitar sound distortion.

Heavy metal performances are characterized by flamboyant, pyrotechnical guitar solos and boundary-defying, defiantly counter-cultural stagecraft. In song lyrics, bands often dwell on imagery of violence, death, unrestrained hedonism, and occult practices, while mocking religious symbols, faith, and conventional themes of love. While many heavy metal bands are far more socially conscious and avoid these extremes, the impact of such lyrics has been debated in the context of shocking outbreaks of violence among youth.
