Disco

Disco is a style of up tempo dance music which combines a fast tempo, Four-on-the-floor rhythms, guitar driven energy, syncopated bass lines, luscious orchestral arrangements, vocals with reverb, and escapist lyrics about love and dancing.

Disco first appeared in the early 1970s in the clubs of New York and has its musical roots in funk and soul. The most famous club was Studio 54. The first audiences were predominantly gay or black. Discotheques played vinyl records on powerful sound systems for non-stop dancing. Elaborate strobe disco lighting systems throbbed to the beat of the music.

Significant Disco Musicians

  • Donna Summer
  • The Bee Gees
  • Gloria Gaynor
  • Diana Ross
  • Chic
